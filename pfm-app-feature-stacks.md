Nonobvious features are explained in (parentheses) on first mention.

## Part 1 — What banks & neobanks are building

The big shift in 2026: banks are embedding AI assistants and predictive money tools directly into their own apps, eroding the gap that standalone budgeting apps used to own.

**Bank of America — Erica** (conversational AI assistant inside the banking app). Answers natural-language questions about balances, cash flow and transactions; surfaces proactive insights (spending alerts, upcoming bills, unusual charges, duplicate charges, subscription price increases); routes to a human agent in one tap. BofA is reusing the same engine across business lines (CashPro Chat, Ask Private Banking). Over 3 billion interactions to date — it's the proof that in-app AI is now table stakes for banks.

**Revolut — AIR** (AI by Revolut, launched April 2026). Answers spending/budgeting/investing questions, can pause recurring payments, gives live FX rates, and performs card controls (freeze card, set spending limits) by chat command. Revolut also has the broader neobank stack: multi-currency accounts, instant spend notifications, budgets, savings vaults, round-ups (spare-change saving — purchases rounded up and the difference auto-saved), and crypto/stock trading.

**Chase, Wells Fargo, Capital One, etc.** are all shipping variations of: AI assistant, predictive cash-flow / "low balance before payday" warnings, automated savings rules, subscription detection, and credit-score monitoring built into the core app.

**What banks are racing toward (the roadmap themes):**
- Conversational AI as the primary interface ("ask, don't navigate").
- Proactive/agentic insights — the app watches in the background and warns or suggests before you ask.
- Predictive cash-flow forecasting (projecting your balance forward to flag shortfalls).
- Personalization at scale (77% of banking leaders call this the top retention driver).
- Embedded financial wellness — credit building, savings nudges, coaching.

The strategic takeaway: when banks give away AI insights for free inside the app users already have, a standalone app has to win on **cross-bank aggregation, neutrality (no product to sell you), depth of budgeting, and couples/multi-account features** that a single bank can't offer.

## Part 2 — App-by-app feature stacks

### A. Premium all-in-one aggregators (the Mint successors)

**Monarch Money**
- Account aggregation across banks, cards, loans, investments, crypto, real estate.
- Auto-categorization with rules; receipt scanning.
- Flex budgeting (spending grouped into three buckets: fixed, non-monthly recurring, and flexible — instead of dozens of line-item categories).
- Goals (savings/debt goals, reworked in 2026).
- Net worth tracking; investment & equity tracking (including stock/equity compensation).
- AI Assistant (natural-language Q&A over your finances).
- Household / couples: unlimited collaborators at no extra cost; Shared Views (label accounts/transactions as "mine," "theirs," "ours").
- Credit-score tracking; connection-health metrics (flags when a bank link breaks); customizable notifications.
- Pricing: Core ~$99.99/yr, Plus ~$199/yr.

**Copilot Money** (iOS/Apple only)
- AI categorization (ML model that learns from your past corrections and shows its top-two guesses for fast fixing).
- Recurring detection (auto-finds subscriptions/bills from transaction patterns, updates as amounts change).
- Investment tracking (brokerages, 401(k), IRA) alongside bank accounts.
- Spending trends, monthly category budgets, net worth.
- Proactive AI assistant (beta) — watches spending in the background, flags unusual charges, suggests category cleanups, asks approval before changing anything (agentic).
- Deep Apple ecosystem integration (widgets, Apple Card/Cash import).
- Pricing: ~$13/mo or ~$95/yr.

**Quicken Simplifi**
- Automation-first tracking: real-time spending plan that projects what's left to spend after bills and savings.
- Watchlists (track spending in custom groupings, e.g. "coffee").
- Bill & subscription tracking, savings goals, net worth, basic investment tracking, custom reports.
- Positioned between PocketGuard (simple) and YNAB (complex).

**Quicken Classic** (desktop software, the legacy power tool)
- Full double-entry-grade budgeting, bill pay, investment tracking with cost-basis and tax reporting.
- Lifetime Planner (long-range retirement projection) and retirement simulator.
- Property/business accounting, rental-property tools.
- Subscription-based; Windows/Mac desktop.

### B. Zero-based & envelope budgeting (proactive "give every dollar a job")

**YNAB (You Need A Budget)**
- Zero-based budgeting (every dollar is assigned a job until income minus assignments equals zero).
- Goal targets per category; age-of-money (how many days old the money you're spending is — a measure of how far ahead you are).
- Bank sync via Plaid (incl. UK/Canada/EU — rare among US apps).
- Reports, net worth, debt payoff tools.
- YNAB Together (share the subscription with up to 5 people).
- Steep learning curve; ~$109/yr.

**EveryDollar** (Ramsey Solutions; relaunched Jan 2026)
- Zero-based budgeting framework tied to Ramsey's "baby steps."
- Margin Finder (scans your budget to surface extra spare money).
- Personalized plans, daily lessons, live group coaching (premium).
- Bank connection, custom reports, recommendations (premium).

**Goodbudget**
- Digital envelope budgeting (allocate income into virtual envelopes per category).
- Manual entry / no bank sync (you key in balances) — appeals to privacy-minded and debt-payoff users.
- Multi-device sync for sharing envelopes with a partner.
- Free tier (limited envelopes); paid ~$10/mo for unlimited.

**Actual Budget** (open-source, self-hostable)
- Envelope/zero-based budgeting, local-first data ownership.
- Optional bank sync via third-party connectors; runs on your own server for full privacy.
- Free if self-hosted.

### C. Subscription & bill management focus

**Rocket Money** (formerly Truebill)
- Subscription detection and one-tap cancellation (cancels unwanted subscriptions for you).
- Bill negotiation (their team contacts your providers to lower bills; success-based fee).
- Budgets, spending insights, net worth, balance/spend alerts.
- Automated savings ("Smart Savings"); credit-score tracking.

**PocketGuard**
- "In My Pocket" (single headline number of safe-to-spend money after bills, goals and budgets).
- Automatic subscription detection; bill tracking with due-date reminders.
- Debt-payoff planner (debt avalanche/snowball strategies).
- Net worth and unlimited categories on premium.

---

### D. Net-worth & wealth/investment trackers

**Empower** (formerly Personal Capital) — core tools free
- Net worth tracker; account aggregation incl. crypto (added 2026).
- Investment Checkup and Fee Analyzer (detects hidden annual fees across your investment accounts).
- Retirement Planner (Monte Carlo-style projection of retirement readiness).
- Basic budgeting/cash-flow. (Monetizes via wealth-management upsell.)

**Kubera** — premium net-worth tracker (~$249/yr)
- Tracks everything: stocks, crypto, real estate, private equity, art, domains, vehicles.
- Connects to thousands of institutions worldwide; multi-currency.
- Beneficiary/"dead man's switch" (auto-shares your portfolio with a trusted person if you go inactive); fast-growing-asset and IRR analytics.

**Origin** — all-in-one wealth + planning (US)
- Net worth, budgeting, investment tracking, plus tax and estate-document tools and human/AI financial-planning guidance bundled in.

---

### E. AI-first & automated-savings apps

**Cleo** — AI chatbot front-end
- Conversational budgeting friend with "roast mode" / "hype mode" (it teases or encourages you about your spending).
- Round-ups and automated savings; cash advances; credit-building.
- Spending breakdowns and forecasts via chat.

**Plum** (UK/EU)
- Smart savings algorithm (analyzes income/spending and auto-moves small affordable amounts to savings every few days).
- Round-ups, savings "rules" (e.g. save £1 every time it rains), investing, subscription detection.

**Albert** (US)
- Automated savings, "Genius" human + AI financial advice, cash advances, budgeting, spending insights.

---

### F. Spreadsheet / power-user / open-source

**Tiller** — spreadsheet-based
- Auto-feeds your transactions into Google Sheets / Excel daily; you keep full control with templates.
- For people who want spreadsheet flexibility without manual data entry.

**Lunch Money** — indie, developer-friendly
- Multi-currency budgeting and crypto tracking; customers in 30+ countries.
- Custom budget periods, net worth, rules, tags, full API access.

**Firefly III / GnuCash / HomeBank / MoneyManager Ex** — free & open-source
- Self-hosted or desktop double-entry accounting and budgeting; complete data ownership, no subscription. For the privacy-maximalist / technical user.

---

### G. Couples & family

**Honeydue**
- Built for couples; connects 20,000+ institutions across 5 countries.
- Granular sharing controls (choose exactly which accounts/balances your partner sees).
- Shared bill reminders, in-app chat on transactions, joint budget categories. Free.

**Monnetta / Spend Pulse / similar** — newer couples-only apps
- Real-time shared budgets so both partners see identical numbers instantly; some are manual-entry / receipt-scan only (no bank linking) for privacy.

(Monarch's "Household" and YNAB Together also serve this need within general apps.)

---

### H. EU / UK & multi-currency (open-banking era)

**Emma** (UK/EU/US)
- Open-banking aggregation; strong subscription tracker (flags forgotten/duplicate subscriptions).
- Budgets, net worth, multiple-account view, "wasting money" detection.

**Snoop** (UK)
- Open-banking aggregation; personalized money-saving "Snoops" (tailored tips and alerts, e.g. cheaper energy/bills).
- Spending alerts, bill-change detection, budgets.

**Spendee**
- Strong spending visualization with custom categories; shared wallets; multi-currency; manual or bank-linked.

**Wallet by BudgetBakers**
- Multi-currency accounts (good for travelers/expats); cash-flow dashboard, budgets, shared accounts, manual + bank sync. CZ-based, strong EU coverage.

**Money Dashboard** (UK), **Plum**, **Revolut** — also in this open-banking cluster.

## Part 3 — The common feature taxonomy (what "a feature stack" is made of)

Across every app above, features cluster into these layers:

1. **Data in:** bank/card/investment/crypto aggregation (via Plaid / Tink / GoCardless / Salt Edge), manual entry, CSV import, receipt scanning.
2. **Organize:** auto-categorization (rules + ML), recurring/subscription detection, merchant enrichment (cleaning cryptic bank descriptions into readable names + logos).
3. **Budget:** zero-based, envelope, flex-buckets, or "safe-to-spend" single number.
4. **See:** dashboards, spending trends, net worth, cash-flow, investment performance, custom reports.
5. **Plan ahead:** goals with milestones, cash-flow forecasting, retirement/longevity planners, debt-payoff planners.
6. **Act:** subscription cancellation, bill negotiation, automated savings/round-ups, alerts & reminders.
7. **Intelligence:** AI assistant (Q&A), proactive/agentic insights, anomaly detection, personalized tips.
8. **Social:** couples/household sharing with permissions, shared budgets, in-app transaction chat.
9. **Trust:** no-ads/no-data-selling stance, data export, credit-score monitoring, security (encryption, 2FA, read-only access).
10. **Reach:** multi-currency, multi-country bank coverage, platform availability (iOS/Android/web/desktop).
